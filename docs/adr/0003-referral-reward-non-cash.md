# Referral Reward is Non-Cash Account Value, Not a Cash Payout

The Referral Reward is granted as **free reservations** (waived Louez commissions) for a pay-as-you-go Referrer, or as **invoice credit** for a subscribed Referrer — never as a cash payout. This is deliberate: the reward stays a **commercial discount on the store's Louez invoice**, not income paid out to the store. It keeps the reward out of "apporteur d'affaires" territory (no invoice the referrer must issue, no VAT to handle, no payout KYC), and it keeps the value inside the ecosystem, improving retention.

## Considered Options

- **Cash payout** via Stripe transfers/payouts. Rejected for launch: triggers apporteur-d'affaires obligations in France (the referrer must invoice us, handle VAT), plus payout KYC and bank collection — heavy build and legal setup for an unproven program.
- **Recurring commission** (a continuing share of the referred store's fees). Rejected for launch: ongoing, hard-to-cap cost, and it belongs to a future external Affiliate Program, not a referral program between existing customers.
- **Non-cash account value** (free reservations / invoice credit). Chosen.

## Consequences

- Free reservations are worth €0 to a **subscribed** Referrer (they pay no per-reservation fee), so the Referrer Reward is **plan-aware**: free reservations when pay-as-you-go, invoice credit when subscribed.
- The reward must be **displayed as "X free reservations ≈ €Y saved"**, never as a withdrawable cash balance, to avoid implying a payment is coming.
- Cash and recurring rewards remain open for a later **Affiliate Program** aimed at external promoters — but only once the deliberately-deferred legal/tax/payout setup is built.
- Program terms must state the reward is non-cash, non-transferable, and non-convertible, with a reserved right to modify or end the program and to void rewards on refund/fraud (clawback).
